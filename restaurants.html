Restaurant Reviews

Eating my way through Logan Square, Chicago.

Notes on places I've eaten in the neighborhood. Not trying to be a critic, just keeping track of what's good and what I'd go back to. Mostly Logan Square, sometimes wandering into neighboring areas when hunger demands it.

Lula Cafe
New American, Farm-to-Table 9/10

Lula has been anchoring Logan Square's dining scene for over two decades now, and there's a reason it's still packed on weekends. The menu changes constantly based on what's coming in from local farms, which means you're never quite sure what you'll get, but you're almost always glad you got it.

The Monday night prix fixe is the move if you want the full experience without completely destroying your wallet. Last time I went, there was this roasted carrot dish with some kind of tahini situation that I'm still thinking about weeks later.

Order the farm egg if it's on the menu. I don't know what they do to it, but it's not like any egg you've made at home.
Last visited: November 2025
Mi Tocaya Antojeria
Modern Mexican 9/10

Diana Davila's place is doing something genuinely interesting with Mexican food that doesn't feel like it's trying too hard to be "elevated" or whatever. The flavors are bold and the presentations are beautiful without being fussy.

The queso fundido with rajas is mandatory. The carnitas are crispy in all the right places. And the mezcal list is deep enough to get you into trouble if you're not careful.

The pork belly tostada is a perfect bite. Crispy, fatty, acidic, all at once.
Last visited: October 2025
Parson's Chicken & Fish
American, Fried Chicken 7/10

Look, sometimes you just want fried chicken and a frozen negroni on a patio, and Parson's delivers exactly that. The chicken is solid, not life-changing, but consistently good. The real draw is the vibe: picnic tables, slushie cocktails, and that feeling of summer even when it's barely warm enough to sit outside.

The hush puppies are better than they need to be. The slaw is fine. The whole experience is greater than the sum of its parts.

Last visited: September 2025
Spinning J
Bakery, Cafe, Pies 8/10

A pie and ice cream shop that also does solid breakfast and lunch. The space is cozy in that way where you want to stay for hours with a book, which is exactly what I've done more times than I'd like to admit.

The pies rotate seasonally and they take them seriously. The chess pie is a staple. The biscuits at brunch are the real deal, flaky and buttery and big enough to share (but you won't want to).

Get a slice of whatever pie looks good and a scoop of ice cream. Don't overthink it.
Last visited: November 2025
Longman & Eagle
Gastropub, American 8/10

The whiskey bar that also happens to serve really good food. The menu leans hearty and rich, which pairs well with the extensive bourbon selection. It's the kind of place where you end up staying longer than planned because the bartender keeps recommending things you haven't tried.

The bone marrow is indulgent in the best way. The burger is one of the better ones in the neighborhood. Brunch gets crowded but the wild boar sloppy joe is worth the wait.

Last visited: October 2025
Bang Bang Pie & Biscuits
Bakery, Southern 7/10

Another pie spot in the neighborhood, with more of a Southern bent. The biscuit sandwiches are the main event here, stuffed with various combinations of eggs, cheese, and meat. The line on weekend mornings can be brutal, but it moves.

The pies are good, maybe not quite as refined as Spinning J, but the portions are generous and the prices are fair. The key lime is a standout.

Last visited: August 2025
Cafe Marie-Jeanne
French Bistro 8/10

A tiny French-ish spot on Humboldt Boulevard that feels like it was transported from some idealized version of Paris. The menu is small and changes often, but everything I've had has been carefully made and thoughtfully seasoned.

The rotisserie chicken is simple and perfect. The natural wine list is interesting without being pretentious. It's the kind of place where you trust the kitchen and just order whatever sounds good.

Sit at the counter if you can. Watching them work in that tiny kitchen is half the experience.
Last visited: November 2025
