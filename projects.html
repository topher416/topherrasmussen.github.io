Mindfulness-Oriented Recovery Enhancement Research

University of Utah, C-MIIND (2022-2024)

Contributed to NIH-funded research on integrating Motivational Interviewing with Mindfulness-Oriented Recovery Enhancement (MORE) for substance use disorder treatment. The research aimed to understand neural mechanisms through which mindfulness practices can address chronic pain and reduce opioid misuse.

My role involved collecting psychophysiological data through Near-Infrared Spectroscopy (NIRS), Electroencephalogram (EEG), and heart rate variability measurements, conducting diagnostic interviews, and coordinating research participants.

This research has significant implications for developing non-pharmacological approaches to addiction treatment and pain management, with particular relevance to addressing the opioid crisis.

Acting Portfolio

Selected Professional Performances (2009-2023)

Over a decade of professional stage acting experience across multiple theatre companies in Salt Lake City, exploring contemporary plays, classic works, and new play development. Key performances include roles in "Egress," "Fun Home," "Harbur Gate," and "The Ice Front."

My approach to acting draws from Michael Chekhov's technique while incorporating elements of mindfulness and somatic awareness. I'm particularly interested in vulnerable, truthful performances that connect audiences to complex emotional experiences.

Title I Arts Education Program

Salt Lake Acting Company (2019-2024)

Led the fundraising and execution of this initiative that brought professional musical theatre experiences to thousands of underserved K-2 schoolchildren through free field trips. The program aimed to introduce theatre arts to children who might otherwise never experience live performance.

During the pandemic, I spearheaded the program's pivot to virtual delivery and subsequently guided its return to in-person implementation. This required developing innovative approaches to engage young audiences through different media while maintaining educational value.

The program included comprehensive educational materials for teachers, interactive elements for students, and curriculum connections to enhance classroom learning through arts integration.

AI-Powered Design & Communications

Third Horizon (2024-Present)

Leading AI research, experimentation, and implementation to advance healthcare communications. This work involves developing innovative approaches to conveying complex healthcare concepts through visual design, content creation, and multimedia strategies.

Projects include designing UI interfaces for healthcare applications, creating infographics and visual aids for white papers, producing video content explaining healthcare policy, and developing strategies to make healthcare information more accessible and engaging.

This interdisciplinary work draws on my background in theater, psychology, and social work to create human-centered designs that effectively communicate complex ideas while maintaining empathy for diverse audiences.
